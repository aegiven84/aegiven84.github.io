Play Therapy

Play therapy is offered with individuals and families and refers to a variety of treament methods and modalities all surrounding play as the central theme for treatment. This technique employs the therapeutic benefits of play and practitioners believe that play has the power to heal, to provide comfort, and to increase understanding of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.

Play therapy empowers children to address and solve their own problems using the natural language of children which is play. Through play therapy children will learn to better communicate their thoughts and feelings, communicate with others in a more productive way, develop problem solving skills, and better cope with thoughts, feelings, and the world around them. The play therapist assists children during the play therapy session to work through their current problems and difficulties using basic element of play and a variety of play materials.

Play therapy can be utilized with a variety of behavioral and emotional concerns and is most appropriate for children ages 3 to 12 but can also be utilized with adolescents and adults.
