Founder | Facilitator, A Time for Art

I'm Deb Ogburn, Founder of A Time for Art. I facilitate workshops, support groups and private sessions that help people restore and revitalize their lives through creative expression in the arts. I work with adults, couples, teens, youth—organizations and businesses, too!

Benefits of Expressive Arts

  • Improved mood
  • Feel more authentic
  • Greater sense of purpose and meaning
  • Adapt more easily to change
  • Take healthy risks
  • Balance your brain by engaging in creative play
  • Develop your ability to improvise
  • Feel more confident and creative
  • Connect more with others

You don't need any artistic talent or skill.

In expressive arts, the focus is not on craft; it's the experience of making and being present with your images that's important. Our "work" is done in the imaginal realm of play and experimentation with various art forms and media. It's kind of like going back to the way we learned as kids, before we embodied all the "shoulds" and "can'ts" and "been-there-done-thats". As your facilitator, I'll guide you through creative processes that have the potential to reveal new possibilities.

Healing the past.

Sometimes there are past traumas that need to be addressed before a lasting change can be made. As a Registered Expressive Arts Therapist with over 12 years of clinical experience, I can help you use the arts as a safe container for strong or difficult feelings, so you can express and release anything that stands in the way of achieving your goals.
